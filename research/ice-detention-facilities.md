## Key Information
- ICE ERO (Enforcement and Removal Operations) oversees civil immigration detention
- Facilities house individuals for immigration proceedings or removal
- ICE detains those subject to mandatory detention or those deemed public safety/flight risk

## How to Locate a Detained Person (NYC Guide)

### ICE Detainee Locator
- Website: locator.ice.gov
- To use the locator, you need:
  - Country of birth AND "A number" OR
  - Country of birth, first name, last name, and date of birth (name must be spelled correctly)

### A-Number (Alien Number)
- An 8 or 9-digit identification number found on most immigration documents
- Follows the letter "A" (e.g., A 000-000-000)
- Can be found on the person's wristband when detained
- If you don't have it, ask your friend/family member for it when you hear from them

### NYC Immigration Legal Support Hotline
- Phone: 1-800-354-0365
- Hours: Monday to Friday, 9AM to 6PM
- Interpretation available in preferred language
- Provides free and confidential immigration legal help

## Communication with Detained Individuals

### Phone Calls
- Detained individuals can make collect calls or use prepaid phone accounts
- Phone systems vary by facility - some use Talton, others use different providers
- Family can set up prepaid accounts to receive calls
- Calls are typically monitored and recorded

### Mail
- Can send letters and photos to the detention facility
- Address must include the person's full name and A-number
- Some facilities have restrictions on what can be sent

### Visitation
- Each facility has its own visitation hours and rules
- Contact the facility directly to confirm hours
- Visitors typically need valid ID
- Visitation hours subject to change

## What Happens After ICE Arrest

### Initial Processing
1. Person is taken into ICE custody
2. Fingerprinting and photographing
3. Background check conducted
4. Custody determination made (release, bond, or detention)

### Notice to Appear (NTA)
- Official document that starts removal proceedings
- Form I-862
- Filed with immigration court by DHS
- Contains:
  - Factual allegations against the person
  - Charges of removability
  - Date and time of hearing (sometimes added later)
  - Location of immigration court

### After NTA is Issued
1. Case assigned to immigration judge
2. Master calendar hearing scheduled
3. Person can apply for relief from removal
4. Individual merits hearing if relief is sought
5. Judge issues decision

### Bond Determination
- ICE may set bond or determine no bond
- Person can request bond hearing before immigration judge
- Judge considers flight risk and danger to community
- Bond amounts typically range from $1,500 to $25,000+
